==========================================================================================
SUPPLEMENTARY ANALYSIS: AREA DEPRIVATION INDEX (ADI) AS ALTERNATIVE PREDICTOR
==========================================================================================

Rationale:
  ADI was developed for healthcare-access research (Singh 2003; Kind & Buckingham,
  NEJM 2018). Unlike SVI (disaster management), ADI measures neighborhood
  socioeconomic disadvantage. Prior work found ADI-associated breast imaging disparities.

Construction:
  County-level ADI via PCA of 6 ACS-derived indicators (58.7% variance explained):
    - % below 150% poverty | % unemployed | % no HS diploma
    - % housing cost-burdened | Median household income (inv.) | % child poverty
  Sources: CDC SVI 2022 (indicator-level) + County Health Rankings 2024
  National percentile rank: 0-100 (higher = more deprived)

  Sample: n = 3,029 counties (rate-eligible with complete ADI data)

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
RESULTS: SVI vs ADI COMPARISON
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  Predictor        Modality IRR      95% CI             p-value    Significant?
  ----------------------------------------------------------------------
  SVI per 10-pctl  CMR      0.992    0.952-1.033        0.681      No
  SVI per 10-pctl  CCT      1.020    0.989-1.053        0.213      No
  ADI per 10-pctl  CMR      0.9373   0.9000-0.9762    0.0018     YES **
  ADI per 10-pctl  CCT      0.9789   0.9490-1.0097    0.1767     No

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Spearman (rate vs ADI): CMR rho=-0.1715 p < 0.0001 | CCT rho=-0.1632 p < 0.0001
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

INTERPRETATION:
  - CMR: ADI SIGNIFICANT (p = 0.0018). Per 10-pctl increase in deprivation,
    CMR facility rate decreases by 6.3% (IRR = 0.9373).
    ADI detects a socioeconomic gradient that SVI (p = 0.68) missed.

  - CCT: ADI NOT significant (p = 0.1767), consistent with SVI null.

  - CONTRIBUTION: SVI misses an access gap that ADI catches for CMR.
    CMR (more specialized, fewer sites) shows socioeconomic sensitivity;
    CCT (more widespread) does not. This supports using ADI in imaging access research.

==========================================================================================
DR. NAEEM CLARIFICATIONS:
==========================================================================================

  1. n = 3,038 in SVI regressions vs 3,144 total counties:
     YES - the 106 dropped counties are exactly those with <1,000 adults aged >=45
     (rate_excluded = 1). These are very low-population counties where per-capita
     rates would be unstable/meaningless.

  2. Table 1 medians are all 0.00:
     YES - this is the zero-inflation. 90.5% of counties have ZERO CMR facilities
     and 82.4% have ZERO CCT facilities. The median (and 75th percentile for CMR)
     are 0 because the vast majority of counties lack any facility. This is itself
     the key finding - cardiac imaging is extremely concentrated.
     Suggested footnote: 'Median of 0.00 reflects that >80% of US counties lack
     any ACR-accredited cardiac imaging facility (zero-inflated distribution).'
